⚙️ Midjourney v7 Parameter Guide

Parameters are added at the end of every Midjourney prompt and dramatically affect the output. Here are the most important ones to know in 2026:

ParameterWhat It DoesBest Values
--v 7Uses the latest Midjourney modelAlways use --v 7
--arAspect ratio — controls image shape16:9 (landscape), 4:5 (portrait), 1:1 (square), 21:9 (cinematic)
--stylizeHow strongly MJ applies its artistic style0-300 (literal), 400-600 (balanced), 750-1000 (artistic)
--style rawMore photorealistic, less AI-lookingUse for portraits and product shots
--chaosHow much variation between outputs0-10 (consistent), 25-50 (varied), 75-100 (wild)
--weirdIntroduces unexpected and surreal elements250-500 (subtle), 1000+ (very strange)
--noNegative prompt — what to exclude--no text, watermark, blurry, oversaturated
--seedLocks in a specific output for reproducibilityAny number — use to recreate a specific image
💡 The most important parameter combination for photorealism: Add --style raw --stylize 500 --v 7 to any portrait or product prompt. This combination removes Midjourney's heavy artistic filter and produces cleaner, more realistic results.

💡 Tips for Better Midjourney Results

1. Specify the Camera and Lens

One of the biggest improvements you can make. Instead of just describing your subject, add the camera: "shot on Sony A7R V with 85mm f/1.4 lens" or "Hasselblad medium format, 80mm". Midjourney understands camera characteristics and applies the appropriate depth of field, rendering quality, and aesthetic.

2. Name the Lighting

Lighting transforms an image. Be specific: "golden hour with warm side lighting", "dramatic volumetric god rays", "neon noir with colored shadows", "overcast soft diffused light". Generic prompts produce generic lighting.

3. Reference Real Artists

Artist references are incredibly powerful. "in the style of Gregory Crewdson" produces cinematic atmospheric photography. "Frank Frazetta" produces epic fantasy illustration. "Annie Leibovitz" produces editorial portraiture. Use them liberally.

4. Use --style raw for Realism

By default Midjourney adds heavy stylization that makes images look obviously AI-generated. Adding --style raw produces much more photorealistic, less artificial results — essential for portraits and product shots.

5. Iterate with Variations

Don't settle for the first grid. Use the V1-V4 buttons to get variations on your favorite result, or use the Re-run button to see completely different interpretations. The best Midjourney results usually come from the 3rd or 4th iteration.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best Midjourney prompts in 2026?
The best Midjourney prompts in 2026 are specific about camera, lighting, mood, color palette, and artist style. They include parameters like --ar, --v 7, --stylize, and --style raw. All 35 prompts on this page are tested on Midjourney v7 and ready to copy.
How do I write a good Midjourney prompt?
A good Midjourney prompt includes: subject description, art style, lighting (golden hour, neon noir, volumetric), camera and lens, color palette, technical quality (8K, ultra-detailed), artist references, and parameters at the end (--ar 16:9 --v 7 --stylize 750). The more specific, the better the result.
What parameters should I use in Midjourney v7?
For Midjourney v7, the most useful parameters are: --v 7 (latest model), --ar for aspect ratio, --stylize 400-750 for most images, --style raw for photorealism, --chaos 10-25 for variation, and --no to exclude unwanted elements. See the parameter table above for a full guide.
Do these prompts work with DALL·E 3 and Flux?
The descriptive prompts (scene, lighting, style, mood) work well with DALL·E 3 and Flux. Remove the Midjourney-specific parameters (--ar, --v, --stylize) when using other generators since they use different syntax. DALL·E 3 responds best to natural language descriptions.
Are these Midjourney prompts free to use commercially?
The prompts themselves are free to use — they're just text. The images you generate depend on your Midjourney subscription. Midjourney Pro and higher plans allow commercial use of generated images. Check Midjourney's terms of service for your specific plan.
