💡 Tips for Better ChatGPT Results

1. Use Role Prompting

Start your prompt with "You are a [expert role]..." This gives ChatGPT a persona to work from and dramatically improves the quality and specificity of the output. Example: "You are a senior software engineer with 15 years of experience..."

2. Be Specific with Placeholders

Every bracket like [YOUR TOPIC] should be replaced with exact details. "Write a blog post about marketing" gets a generic response. "Write a 1,200-word blog post about TikTok marketing for e-commerce brands selling women's clothing, targeting 25-35 year old buyers" gets something usable.

3. Specify the Output Format

Tell ChatGPT exactly what you want back. "Give me 5 bullet points", "Write this as a table", "Format this as a numbered list with bold headers". The more specific the format request, the more usable the output.

4. Use the Iteration Trick

After the first response, follow up with: "Now make it 30% more specific" or "Rewrite this but more conversational" or "Give me 3 alternative versions of this". ChatGPT gets dramatically better on second and third passes.

5. Chain Prompts for Complex Tasks

Don't try to do everything in one prompt. Break complex tasks into steps — research first, then outline, then draft, then refine. Each step builds on the last for much higher quality outputs.

💡 The one thing most people miss: ChatGPT is a conversation, not a search engine. The best results come from iterating — starting with a solid prompt, reviewing the output, then refining with follow-up instructions.
