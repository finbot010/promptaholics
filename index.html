📚 Free Guide

Learn to Prompt Like a Pro

Master the art of AI prompting. These techniques work with ChatGPT, Midjourney, Claude, Gemini and every other AI tool.

🎯
Be Specific & Detailed
Vague prompts get vague answers. The more context and detail you provide, the better and more useful the AI's response will be.
  • Include who, what, where, why, and how
  • Specify the format you want (list, paragraph, table)
  • Give the target audience and desired tone
  • Add a word count or length target
❌ Weak prompt
Write a blog post about coffee.
click to copy

✅ Strong prompt
Write a 1200-word SEO blog post about the health benefits of black coffee for busy professionals aged 30-45. Use an engaging hook, 5 subheadings, and end with a clear CTA to try a morning coffee ritual.
click to copy
🎭
Assign a Role or Persona
Telling the AI who to "be" dramatically improves output quality. It activates relevant knowledge and sets the right tone automatically.
  • Start with "Act as a [expert role]..."
  • Be specific about experience level
  • Combine role with audience: "senior lawyer explaining to a layperson"
  • Use fictional roles for creative tasks
✅ Role prompt
Act as a senior UX designer with 10 years of SaaS experience. Review this onboarding flow and identify the 3 biggest friction points that cause user drop-off.
click to copy
📋
Use the Chain-of-Thought Technique
Ask the AI to think step by step before answering. This dramatically improves accuracy on complex tasks, math, and reasoning problems.
  • Add "think step by step" to any prompt
  • Ask it to show its reasoning
  • Use "First... then... finally..." structure
  • Ask it to consider pros and cons before deciding
✅ Chain-of-thought prompt
Should I quit my job to freelance? Think through this step by step, considering: my financial runway, marketable skills, risk tolerance, and market demand. Then give me a clear recommendation.
click to copy
🔄
Iterate & Refine
The first response is rarely the best. Great prompting is a conversation — refine, adjust, and build on each response to get exactly what you need.
  • "Make it more concise / detailed / casual / formal"
  • "Rewrite this but focus more on [specific aspect]"
  • "Give me 3 alternative versions of the opening"
  • "What's missing from this that would make it better?"
✅ Refinement prompt
This is good but too formal. Rewrite it in a conversational tone, cut it by 30%, and make the opening hook more surprising. Keep all the key data points.
click to copy
📐
Specify Format & Structure
Tell the AI exactly how to structure its response. This saves editing time and makes outputs immediately usable.
  • Request bullet points, numbered lists, or tables
  • Specify headers: "use H2 headings for each section"
  • Ask for JSON, markdown, or plain text output
  • Define sections: "include: intro, 3 tips, conclusion"
✅ Format prompt
Summarize this meeting in a table with 3 columns: Decision Made, Owner, Deadline. Then add a separate section with the 3 biggest risks discussed and how we plan to mitigate each.
click to copy
🖼️
Image Prompts: The Formula
Great image prompts follow a proven formula that consistently produces stunning results in Midjourney, DALL-E, and Stable Diffusion.
  • Subject — what/who is in the image
  • Style — photorealistic, oil painting, anime, etc.
  • Lighting — golden hour, studio, dramatic
  • Camera — 85mm lens, wide angle, macro
  • Mood — cinematic, dreamy, gritty
  • Quality — 8K, award-winning, trending
✅ Image formula prompt
Portrait of an elderly fisherman, photorealistic, golden hour lighting, shot on 85mm f/1.4 lens, shallow depth of field, weathered face with kind eyes, cinematic mood, award-winning photography --ar 4:5 --v 7

⚡
Use Constraints Creatively
Constraints spark creativity. Giving the AI limitations forces it to think more inventively and produce more original output.
  • "Explain this using only analogies from cooking"
  • "Write this without using the word 'very' or 'really'"
  • "Give me 10 ideas, but they must all be free to implement"
  • "Write a marketing email in under 100 words"
✅ Constraint prompt
Explain blockchain technology using only analogies from everyday banking that a 60-year-old who has never used a smartphone would immediately understand. Maximum 150 words.
click to copy
🧪
Ask for Multiple Options
Never settle for the first answer. Ask for variations, alternatives, and options — then choose the best or combine elements from several.
  • "Give me 5 versions with different tones"
  • "Write 3 alternatives: conservative, bold, and unconventional"
  • "What are the top 3 approaches to this problem?"
  • "Create A/B test versions of this headline"
✅ Multiple options prompt
Write 5 email subject lines for our product launch. Version A: curiosity-driven. Version B: benefit-led. Version C: urgency. Version D: social proof. Version E: question format. Each under 50 characters.
