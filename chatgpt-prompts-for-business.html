25 ChatGPT Prompts for Business That Drive Real Results (2026)

📅 May 2026⏱ 9 min read✍️ Promptaholics Team

These aren't generic "write me an email" prompts. These are the specific, structured ChatGPT prompts that entrepreneurs, marketers and business owners use to save hours every week and produce work that actually converts.

We've organized them into 5 categories: Marketing & Sales, Strategy & Planning, Operations, Content Creation and Customer Success. Each prompt is copy-ready — just replace the bracketed text with your details.

Marketing & Sales Prompts

Prompt 1 · Cold Email
High-Converting Cold Email Generator
You are an elite B2B sales copywriter. Write a cold outreach email for [YOUR COMPANY] targeting [TARGET ROLE] at [TARGET COMPANY TYPE]. Product/Service: [WHAT YOU SELL] Key benefit: [MAIN VALUE PROPOSITION] Pain point you solve: [SPECIFIC PROBLEM] Requirements: - Subject line that gets opened (under 50 chars) - Opening line that references their specific situation - 2-3 sentences on the problem you solve - One clear CTA — not "let's hop on a call" but something specific - Under 150 words total - No jargon or buzzwords Write 3 versions: direct, story-based, and question-led.
💡 Best for: sales teams, founders doing outbound, freelancers pitching clients
Prompt 2 · Competitor Analysis
Competitor Analysis Framework
Act as a senior business strategist. Conduct a thorough competitor analysis for [YOUR BUSINESS] in the [INDUSTRY] market. My main competitors: [LIST 3-5 COMPETITORS] My product/service: [DESCRIPTION] My target customer: [CUSTOMER PROFILE] Analyze each competitor across: 1. Pricing strategy and positioning 2. Key differentiators and strengths 3. Weaknesses and gaps in their offering 4. Marketing channels they dominate 5. Customer reviews — what do customers love/hate? Then identify: 3 opportunities my business can exploit, 2 threats I need to defend against, and my ideal positioning statement based on the gaps you found.
💡 Best for: strategy sessions, investor decks, new product launches
Prompt 3 · Social Proof
Customer Testimonial Transformer
You are a conversion copywriter. Transform this raw customer feedback into powerful social proof for multiple formats: Raw feedback: [PASTE CUSTOMER FEEDBACK] Product/service: [YOUR OFFERING] Customer type: [WHO THEY ARE] Create: 1. A punchy 1-line quote (under 20 words) for ads 2. A 3-sentence case study format (problem → solution → result) 3. A star review format for Google/Trustpilot 4. A LinkedIn recommendation format 5. A before/after story format for Instagram Keep the authentic voice but make each version optimized for its platform.
💡 Best for: marketing teams, ecommerce brands, SaaS companies

Strategy & Planning Prompts

Prompt 4 · Business Plan
90-Day Business Growth Plan
Act as a growth strategist who has scaled 3 businesses past $1M ARR. Create a 90-day growth plan for my business: Business: [BUSINESS NAME/TYPE] Current revenue: [MONTHLY REVENUE] Target: [GOAL IN 90 DAYS] Team size: [NUMBER OF PEOPLE] Main channel: [HOW YOU GET CUSTOMERS] Biggest bottleneck: [YOUR MAIN CHALLENGE] Structure the plan as: - Days 1-30: Foundation (what to fix/build first) - Days 31-60: Scaling (what to double down on) - Days 61-90: Optimization (what to automate/systemize) Include specific weekly actions, KPIs to track, and tools to implement. Be brutally honest about what's not working.
💡 Best for: entrepreneurs, startup founders, small business owners
Prompt 5 · Pricing Strategy
Pricing Strategy Analyzer
You are a pricing strategist. Help me optimize the pricing for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Current price: [CURRENT PRICE] Cost to deliver: [YOUR COST] Target customer: [CUSTOMER TYPE] Main competitors' prices: [COMPETITOR PRICING] Value delivered: [SPECIFIC OUTCOMES/RESULTS] Analyze and recommend: 1. Is my current pricing leaving money on the table? 2. What pricing model fits best (flat fee, subscription, usage, tiered)? 3. 3 pricing options I could offer (good/better/best) 4. The psychological pricing techniques I should use 5. How to justify a price increase without losing customers Give me the specific price points you recommend and why.
💡 Best for: SaaS founders, consultants, service businesses

Content Creation Prompts

Prompt 6 · Email Campaign
7-Email Launch Campaign
You are a direct response email copywriter who has generated over $10M in email revenue. Create a 7-email launch campaign for: Product: [PRODUCT NAME] Price: [PRICE] Launch date: [DATE] Target audience: [WHO BUYS THIS] Main benefit: [TOP TRANSFORMATION] Biggest objection: [MAIN REASON PEOPLE DON'T BUY] Write all 7 emails with subject lines, preview text and body copy: 1. Teaser (7 days before) — create anticipation 2. Story (5 days before) — build connection and credibility 3. Value (3 days before) — demonstrate results 4. Social proof (1 day before) — crush objections 5. Launch day — open cart with urgency 6. Objection handler (2 days after) — address hesitations 7. Last chance (final day) — scarcity close Each email under 300 words. Conversational, not corporate.
💡 Best for: product launches, course creators, ecommerce brands
Prompt 7 · LinkedIn Content
Viral LinkedIn Post Generator
You are a LinkedIn content strategist with 50K+ followers. Write 5 high-engagement LinkedIn posts about [YOUR TOPIC/NICHE]. My audience: [WHO FOLLOWS YOU] My expertise: [YOUR BACKGROUND] Content goal: [BUILD AUTHORITY / GENERATE LEADS / GROW FOLLOWERS] For each post include: - Hook (first line that stops the scroll) - Body (3-5 punchy paragraphs, one idea per paragraph) - CTA (comment, share, or click) - 3-5 relevant hashtags Mix these formats: personal story, hot take, step-by-step tip, contrarian opinion, and industry insight. No corporate speak. Write like a real person.
💡 Best for: B2B founders, consultants, personal brand builders

Operations Prompts

Prompt 8 · SOPs
Standard Operating Procedure Builder
Act as an operations consultant. Create a detailed Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for [SPECIFIC PROCESS] in my [BUSINESS TYPE]. Process overview: [DESCRIBE WHAT NEEDS TO HAPPEN] Team members involved: [LIST ROLES] Tools/software used: [LIST TOOLS] Current problems with this process: [WHAT GOES WRONG] Create an SOP that includes: 1. Purpose and scope 2. Roles and responsibilities 3. Step-by-step process (numbered, clear, specific) 4. Decision points and what to do in each case 5. Quality checks and success metrics 6. Common mistakes to avoid 7. Escalation path when things go wrong Format it so a new hire could follow it on day one.
💡 Best for: scaling teams, operations managers, agency owners

Customer Success Prompts

Prompt 9 · Customer Objections
Sales Objection Handler
You are a sales trainer who has coached 500+ reps. Generate comprehensive objection handling scripts for [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Common objections I face: 1. "It's too expensive" 2. "I need to think about it" 3. "We're happy with our current solution" 4. "Send me some information and I'll get back to you" 5. [ADD YOUR SPECIFIC OBJECTION] For each objection provide: - Acknowledge the concern (empathy first) - Reframe the objection - Ask a discovery question to understand the real concern - The response that moves the conversation forward - A closing question Include both phone/video call scripts and written response templates.
💡 Best for: sales teams, founders closing deals, customer success managers
Prompt 10 · Customer Research
Customer Interview Script Generator
Act as a UX researcher and customer development expert. Create a customer interview script for [YOUR BUSINESS]. Product/service: [WHAT YOU SELL] Interview goal: [WHAT YOU WANT TO LEARN] Customer segment: [WHO YOU'RE INTERVIEWING] Generate: 1. Opening (build rapport, set expectations) — 2 minutes 2. Background questions (understand their context) — 5 questions 3. Problem discovery (find pain points) — 5 questions 4. Solution exploration (test your assumptions) — 5 questions 5. Willingness to pay probe — 3 questions 6. Closing (next steps, referrals) — 2 minutes Include follow-up probes for each question. Format as a conversational guide, not a rigid script.
💡 Best for: product teams, founders doing discovery, market researchers
